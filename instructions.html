Instructions

DTecBiz 2026 – SLIIT Kandy UNI

Instructions for Chairpersons

The chairperson is responsible for introducing each speaker, ensuring adherence to scheduled timing, facilitating discussion between the speakers and audience, and reporting the outcome of each session.

  • All chairpersons should arrive 20 minutes prior to their session.
  • We strongly encourage you to take advantage of the welcome reception/dinner, lunch, and evening events to meet the speakers in your session.
  • Before the speakers begin to present their research papers, introduce yourself and the session, and make any announcements/session rules.
  • We encourage you to introduce each presenter at the commencement of each presentation (it is the responsibility of each presenter to contact the chairperson to find out details on the session arrangements).
  • Session chairs are required to review each presentation and be present.
  • The programme is organized into a series of themed sessions, chaired by a nominated chairperson. The sessions are designed to give an opportunity for each speaker to present a brief summary of their paper, while also providing time for discussion.

Instructions for Speakers

  • All speakers should arrive at the session room 20 minutes prior to the start of their session. Please make the chairperson aware of your attendance before the commencement of the session.
  • Give your presentation to the technical support person available in the session room and save your presentation on the available PC or laptop in your session room before the session starts.
  • We strongly encourage you to take advantage of the welcome reception/dinner, lunch, and evening events to meet your chairpersons to receive important information on session arrangements and also to meet fellow participants in your session.

Session (Presentation) Details

  • Sessions are presented in English and we do not have translation facilities.
  • Keynote lectures have been allocated altogether 25 minutes; 20 minutes for the presentation and 5 minutes for the discussion.
  • Each session starts with a key paper. 20 minutes are allocated for the key paper presentation: 15 minutes for presentation and 5 minutes for discussion.

Other Papers

8 minutes are allowed for each presentation and 7 minutes for discussion, altogether 15 minutes. However, session duration may vary depending on the number of presentations, and accordingly your chairperson will confirm the duration of the presentation at the beginning of the session. The chairperson of your session will terminate your presentation when the given time is over. Therefore, rehearse your talk to make sure that you have enough time for your presentation. As a guide, we recommend the following structure:

  • Background
  • Objectives and aim
  • Methodology
  • Results
If the paper is presented by a substitute, he/she must be familiar with the subject being presented to answer questions from the audience.

Audio-Visual Aids

  • In order to ensure that sessions are run in a timely manner, each speaker is limited to a maximum of 10 minutes of talk time; it is usually only possible to present a maximum of about one slide per minute.
  • All presentation rooms will be equipped with a PC and a data projector.
  • PowerPoint presentations can be read from CD, DVD and memory sticks. We strongly recommend you bring your presentation in two different forms of storage media.
  • If animations or video clips are used, they have to work within PowerPoint — do NOT rely on additional software.
  • If you are using animations/video clips please contact the registration desk to arrange a trial on a conference PC & projector. A technical support person will be available in the room before the session commencement to assist speakers in operating the equipment.
